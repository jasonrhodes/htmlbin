Two Goals for the Week

Goal 1
Leave with every M2 workstream scoped, epics defined, and owners assigned.

Everyone should be able to work independently on their stream for the next 4 weeks. Each session is about getting to that clarity.

Goal 2
Make v2 compelling enough that users choose to switch.

We can trade stability and feature completeness for UX quality and v2 differentiation. Every decision should serve what makes v2 better than v1 for users.

Why v2?

Open System

Maximum flexibility. Guided UX exists alongside powerful advanced modes. Never block users from their goals.

Real Alerts-as-Data

Append-only rule events replace mutating documents. Full alert history, deeper analysis, nothing lost.

ES|QL Integration

ES|QL-first paradigm. Every rule connects to every ES|QL-powered feature across the Elastic ecosystem.

Actions Over Noise

Notification policies decouple actions from rules. Configurable lifecycle. Reusable, composable, quiet when it should be.

v1 and v2 coexist. Migration is user-driven. The incentive proves itself.

What Users Will Notice

  • Episodes — full lifecycle tracking: pending, active, recovering, inactive. Not just on/off.
  • Custom recovery — real hysteresis, not "absence of signal." Define what recovery actually means.
  • Rule-on-rules — query .rule-events to detect cascading failures across rule boundaries.
  • Notification policies — group by episode, status, or digest. Throttle independently. Reuse everywhere.
  • Alert history — every execution persisted in .rule-events. Full audit trail, queryable.
  • Data preservation — data.* fields carried through the entire alert lifecycle via KEEP.
